BENTOTA TURTLE HATCHERY

Bentota Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka is a conservation institution focusing on sea turtle preservation, rehabilitation, and protection, situated in Induruwa, a town that is around 62 kilometers south of Sri Lanka's capital city of Colombo on the island's southwest shore.


Conservation Efforts, Egg Collection And Incubation


Conservation Efforts

Bentota Turtle Hatchery safeguards sea turtles and eggs from threats like poaching, habitat damage, and pollution, ensuring secure hatching.

Egg Collection And Incubation

Eggs collected from shore are carefully unearthed and reburied in secure areas, enhancing hatchling survival by protecting them from natural and human hazards.


Conservation Education And Release Of Hatchings


Conservation Education

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery promotes sea turtle conservation through educational sessions, educating visitors on life cycles, dangers, and conservation techniques.

Release Of Hatchings

Visitors witness newborn turtles hatching, a crucial stage on their journey to maturity.


Rehabilation Of Injured Turtles


Rehabilation Of Injured Turtles

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery treats injured sea turtles from fishing nets, boat collisions, and human actions until healthy enough release.
